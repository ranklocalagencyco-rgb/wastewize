# WasteWize UK — Mission & Brand Reference

> Use this document when writing copy, briefing AI tools, or reviewing page content. It captures the brand's purpose, voice, factual anchors, visual identity, and ideal customer profile so everything stays consistent.

---

## Mission

To make responsible waste management straightforward for UK businesses and homeowners — delivering full compliance, genuine environmental impact, and a hassle-free service every time.

---

## Core Purpose

WasteWize UK exists to close the gap between "getting rid of stuff" and doing it properly. Too many businesses use unlicensed carriers, lose waste transfer notes, or unknowingly expose themselves to legal liability. We fix that — with licensed collections, documented disposal, and a 99% landfill diversion rate.

---

## What We Stand For

**Compliance first.** Every job is covered by our EA Upper-Tier Waste Carrier Licence (CBDU335711). Clients receive a digital Waste Transfer Note on every single collection — no exceptions.

**Zero to landfill as the default.** We currently divert 99% of all collected material from landfill through circular economy processing: wood to biomass, metal to smelters, rubble to secondary aggregate, WEEE to registered contractors, green waste to PAS 100 compost facilities.

**Transparency over promises.** We don't talk about sustainability in vague terms. Every claim is backed by a licence number, a stat, or a process the client can audit.

**Straightforward pricing and service.** No grey-area charges, no unlicensed subcontractors, no fly-tipping risk passed to the client.

---

## The Brand Voice

- **Direct, not corporate.** Say what we do, not what we "strive to deliver."
- **Confident, not boastful.** Let the credentials (EA licence, 99% diversion rate, founded 2018) do the work.
- **Practical, not preachy.** Sustainability is a feature of the service, not a lecture.
- **Human, not robotic.** We're a West London logistics business that cares about doing the job right — not a faceless compliance machine.

Avoid: vague superlatives ("leading provider"), passive constructions ("waste is handled"), jargon without explanation ("duty of care" always gets a one-line follow-up).

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### ICP 1 — The Property Professional

**Who:** Landlords, letting agents, estate agents, and property managers in London and the Home Counties. Typically manages multiple properties and has waste arising regularly from tenancy changeovers, refurbishments, and end-of-tenancy clearances.

**Company size:** Solo operators through to agencies managing 50–500 units.

**Key pain points:**
- Previous experience with unlicensed carriers who left them legally exposed (no waste transfer note).
- Tight turnaround pressure — voids cost money, so they need same-week or next-day availability.
- Inconsistent pricing from man-and-van operators; unexpected add-on charges.
- Fly-tipping risk on their land from rogue disposal.

**What they care about:**
- Speed and reliability above everything.
- A proper paper trail (WTN) so they're covered if the council or Environment Agency ever asks questions.
- A single point of contact they can trust across multiple properties.
- Competitive pricing, particularly if they can set up an account for repeat work.

**Messaging that resonates:** "Same-week collections. Full compliance paperwork every time. One call, sorted."

**Services most relevant:** End of Tenancy Clearance, House Clearance, Office Clearance, Fly Tipping Removal.

---

### ICP 2 — The Construction & Refurbishment Contractor

**Who:** Small to mid-size building contractors, fit-out companies, and sole-trader builders carrying out residential refurbs, commercial strip-outs, or new builds across London and the Home Counties.

**Company size:** 1–50 employees. Often a sole trader or small limited company.

**Key pain points:**
- Skip hire is slow (permit wait times), restrictive (weight/material limits), and often blocked by road access on London streets.
- Site waste compliance is a legal obligation they can't afford to ignore — an unlicensed carrier puts their contract, insurance, and CIS registration at risk.
- They need to move waste fast to keep the site clear and the job on schedule.
- Asbestos and hazardous materials need specialist handling they can't manage themselves.

**What they care about:**
- Reliable same-day or next-day turnaround without the skip hire logistics.
- Full site compliance docs (WTNs, consignment notes for hazardous waste).
- Someone who can handle mixed loads, segregated materials, and specialist streams (rubble, timber, WEEE, asbestos) all under one roof.
- Transparent pricing so they can quote the client accurately.

**Messaging that resonates:** "No permit, no waiting. We load it, we document it, we dispose of it legally."

**Services most relevant:** Builders Waste Removal, Skip Hire Alternative, Strip Out & Demolition, Rubble & Wood Removal, Asbestos Removal, Scrap Metal Collection.

---

### ICP 3 — The Office & Facilities Manager

**Who:** Office managers, operations directors, and facilities leads at SMEs and corporate occupiers across London — particularly in Central, West, and North London. Responsible for relocations, office refits, downsizing, or routine commercial waste contracts.

**Company size:** 10–500 employees. Typically works within a company that has compliance and sustainability reporting requirements.

**Key pain points:**
- WEEE and confidential waste need to be handled with a clear chain of custody — data protection liability is real.
- Their company's sustainability reporting requires evidence of landfill diversion, not just "we recycled."
- Standard commercial waste contractors don't handle clearances — they need someone who can do both scheduled collections and one-off large clearances.
- Out-of-hours or weekend availability to avoid disrupting the business.

**What they care about:**
- GDPR-compliant confidential waste destruction with certificates of destruction.
- WEEE transfer notes for every load.
- Evidence of high landfill diversion rates for ESG/sustainability reports.
- Flexibility (out-of-hours, short-notice, account billing).
- A professional service that won't embarrass them in front of their own clients or staff.

**Messaging that resonates:** "Every load documented. 99% landfill diversion. Certificates issued on the day."

**Services most relevant:** Office Clearance, Confidential Waste, Electrical Equipment (WEEE), Commercial Clearance.

---

### ICP 4 — The Homeowner or Estate Executor

**Who:** Homeowners doing major clearances after bereavement, downsizing, or renovation. Estate executors managing a property clearance on behalf of a deceased relative. Often one-time customers but high-value jobs with emotional stakes.

**Company size:** N/A — individual consumer.

**Key pain points:**
- Unsure who to trust — the market is full of unlicensed "man with a van" operators who may fly-tip.
- Don't know what a skip permit involves or whether one is even needed.
- Often under time pressure (probate, property sale, renovation schedule).
- Want the job done sensitively and without hassle, particularly in bereavement cases.

**What they care about:**
- Peace of mind that the waste will be disposed of legally and responsibly.
- Not having to do any heavy lifting or organise multiple contractors.
- Clear upfront pricing with no nasty surprises.
- Proof that they won't be liable if something goes wrong.

**Messaging that resonates:** "We handle everything. Licensed, insured, fully documented — so you don't have to worry."

**Services most relevant:** House Clearance, Residential Waste, Garden Refuse, Skip Hire Alternative, End of Tenancy Clearance.
